God's leading

Manoj Pradhananga (Director of LTD/NCFN Nepal)

Date: Februrary 2024

God’s leading has been always a theme in the ministry of LTD of NCF Nepal. Early this year in our staff meeting, we were meditating on this theme the life of Joseph and Mary during the early childhood life of Jesus. We could find out God’s clear guidance and thus protection and provision in their life as Joseph and Mary migrat ed to Egypt and back to Galilee. We were able to easily relate the lessons to our ministry in LTD. How do we know God’s leading in our ministry? A couple of signs indicate God’s leading.
Firstly, Jesus said that we would know the tree by its fruit. “Thus, by their fruit, you will recognize them” (Matt 7:20). We hear the stories of our former trainees and know how their ministry is reconciling marriages, leading people to Christ, maturing believers, so now they are confident to share the Word of God in the church and small groups, and more importantly, applying the learning.
Secondly, we see integrity, diligence, joy, and commitment in the life and ministry of the staff team. They have sacrificed more than what LTD has provided. Supporting one another is being practiced more and more.
Thirdly, God has provided the resources needed for the ministry in a timely way. A new partner comes in at a time when a faithful partner stops supporting the ministry. A ministry leader leaves for higher study and another leader comes in at the right time. A volunteer joins to write a training manual that was pending for several years because of a lack of both human and financial re sources.
Fourthly, God’s wisdom in the crisis. Certainly, we also faced a lot of challenges and difficult times. We sensed confidence and promptness while we made tough and painful decisions.
The examples of God’s leading above are not prescriptive but describe our experiences walking with Him. It helps to under stand the ministry and leadership in perspective. As you read this newsletter my wish is that God may give you a perspective to understand His loving guidance in your life and ministry. My special thanks to all who have contributed towards LTD ministry and my prayer is for the potential partners that God may give you a desire to consider supporting the LTD ministry.

The Challenge for Change

Manoj Pradhananga (Director of LTD/NCFN Nepal)

Date: September 2023

The context in which we do mission is changing. The strategy that worked 20 years before no longer works. The common tendency of doing ministry is to continue the way we have been doing. Of course, LTD has changed a lot in the last 17 years, particularly after Covid-19. Our recent impact evaluation clearly showed the need for change in order to grow. We are prayerfully planning to develop a strategic plan this year which we are committed to follow. We are yearning for change. However, we see the chal lenges are overwhelming, and change is not easy.
There is constant hostility growing against the Christians in Nepal communities. An anti-Christian spirit is being created and promoted by a group of people. The recent updates (Aug 2023) by UNITED STATES COMMISSION on INTERNATIONAL RELIGIOUS FREEDOM advocates that the constitution of Nepal doesn’t fully protect Nepali Christians. The Nepal government has a lot of legal restrictions to stop the growth of Christianity. The impact of media on the lives of people has been so influential that without the use of these tools, the ministry is not going to be sustained. However, we don’t have enough resources (human, financial, technical, and other resources) to fully utilize the media. We also have internal challenges. There is unhealthy heavy competition between Christian ministries. We claim to believe in partnership but too often fail to practise it. Thus, our resources are scattered, failing to work together to make optimal use of them.
I have mentioned only a handful of the challenges LTD is facing, though there are many more like Goliath. We require change in order to thrive. Although not easy, change is a sign of growth, and we want to grow. Sustainable change requires sacrifice, commitment, and passion, as well as information and skills. It requires the development of a culture of change, a good team, and structure. LTD is standing at the crossroads of change. LTD requires a strong heart like David. LTD requires a strategy like what David used to kill Goliath. LTD requires your constant prayer and all kinds of support to bring change in Nepal and beyond.

LTD in a new direction with the same vision

Manoj Pradhananga (Director of LTD/NCFN Nepal)

Date: Februrary 2023

Leadership Training Department (LTD) of NCF Nepal completed its training impact evaluation of 15 years of service in 2022. The figures show that it has equipped 729 men and women for church leadership, 900 women for women leadership, and 234 couples for family life by the end of 2020. The figures also report that 80-90% of trained men and women are in active ministry and some kind of leadership positions.
The qualitative research findings clearly demonstrate the former trainees are continuing their ministries in their respective places, substantially contributing to the growth of their churches. The research shows that the top 3 strengths of LTD training are: offered-subjects, teachers, and management. Interestingly, both qualitative and quantitative research findings are consistent with qualitative findings, explaining the reasons. The former trainees reported that they have experienced improvements in their spiritual life, relationships, and ministry efficiency. They have also planted hundreds of new churches or small groups and strengthened the local churches through discipleship.
LTD continues to seek to strengthen young but fast-growing Nepali churches through leadership development in rural and remote Nepal. We stick to the same mission: training pastors and lay leaders, women, and families from rural Nepal. The current impact evaluation has provided us with some clear directions for the change in our strategy and method. LTD is moving now to modular and online trainings while continuing with a smaller number of residential training events. It is now reconnecting and further developing with former trainees. In order to increase local resources for income, LTD is trying to tap resources from former trainees and their sending churches. Nearly 30% of our trainees have indicated that they are willing to support us financially.
The information above is just a glimpse of what God is doing in and through the LTD ministry. God who called us to this ministry is faithful. I Thessalonians 5:24 reads, “The one who calls you is faithful, and he will do it.” However, the context is different today and through God’s Spirit and wisdom, we are developing a new strategy for the next couple of years.
